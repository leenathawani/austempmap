Global Life Expectancy

Life Expectancy refers to the number of years a person is expected to live, and is an indication of the overall mortality level of the population. Longevity is affected by various factors such as gender, genetics, hygiene levels, access to healthcare, crime rate, etc. and can therefore be significantly different in different parts of the world. These visualisations depict the variation in life expectancy across the different countries and continents around the globe.

Life Expectancy By Continent

There is quite a large variation amongst not only continents, but also the many countries in the same continent. These large deviations are due to various factors such as genetics, access to healthcare, diet and nutrition, exercise and lifestyle, crime rate, amongst others. Since these factors differ in different countries, there is bound to be an impact on life expectancy.

Top 15 Countries

Countries in Asia form the Top 5 Countries in the world with highest Life expectancy, despite Asia only being third in terms of highest average life expectancy. European countries take up majority of the Top 15, which is in line with the continent having the highest average life expectancy in the world. A noteworthy mention to Australia, being the only country outside these 2 continents in the Top 15.

Life Expectancy by Continent

Europe has consistenly had the highest average life expectancy compared to all other countries, at 78.29 years in 2020. The Americas are not far behind with an average Life expectancy of about 3 years below, with this trend going downwards until an average life expectancy of 65.17 years in Africa. This is a substantial difference of more than 13 years, indicating a clear distinction.

Where do they stand?

The ranking of continents in terms of highest life expectancy has remained the same throughout history with the sole exception of 2005. In 2005, Asia and America exchanged places, as Asia went up to having the third highest Life Expectancy and the Americas went down a place. This wasn't short lived, as this trend continues til date.
